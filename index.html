1. Essay that focuses on the common threads of these approaches

Derek Muller and Carol Dweck are both education experts. They present different theories on how about but both seem to agree with the fact that the presentation of the knowledge should not only relating the information. For Derek Muller, he makes sure to acknowledge the wrong answer first so that people are engaged with their misconceptions. He then give the right answer with leading questions, so to tease the mind to start piecing together the answer. With Carol Dweck, she believes in praising the work it takes to learn the answers, she would give a student a challenging assignment and praise them for work at it, rather that giving an easy assignment and wanting a student to get 100%. Both, Derek Muller and Carol Dweck want students to push to higher knowledge by working with what they have and acknowledging where they started at. Derek Muller does this by show misconceptions, and Carol Dweck does this by praising the work it takes to grow. Both, in different ways, want people to acknowledge were they started from and be excited for the chance to grow.
